Fintech · Enterprise Senior UI/UX Designer

Modernising a fintech's agent operations

A leading asset manager ran its entire Unit Trust and PRS agent operation on software nearly fifteen years old. I led the design of a single responsive platform to replace it — a role-based home for the back-office team to run the agent network, and for thousands of agents to run their own business.

Role

Senior UI/UX Designer

Industry

Fintech · Asset management

Platform

Responsive web app

Overview

The client is a leading asset manager whose business is sold through a network of licensed agents. The platform manages those agents and the products they distribute — Unit Trust (UT) and the Private Retirement Scheme (PRS) — covering the full agent lifecycle, from onboarding and licensing to day-to-day business, for both the client's staff and the agents themselves.

The goal of the revamp was simple to say and demanding to deliver: retire the ageing legacy system, remove the repetitive manual work and in-person paperwork it forced on everyone, and move the whole operation online — where it could finally be tracked. One modern platform, built to grow with the business.

The problem

A regulated agent network was being run on fifteen-year-old software — by hand, and on paper.

The legacy system still worked, but it shaped how everyone behaved around it: slow, manual, and impossible to see into. Each limitation carried a real cost — for agents, for staff, or for the business.

What we were up against

Ageing legacy software

Nearly fifteen years old, costly to maintain, and holding the business back from working in a modern, online way.

Paper, in person

Onboarding and routine requests meant filling forms by hand, in person — tying activity to a place and a delay.

Repetitive manual tasks

Staff and agents repeated the same time-consuming steps by hand — hours better spent on customers.

No way to track anything

With little visibility into applications or status, people chased updates by phone and email — and nothing could be measured.

Slow agent onboarding

Bringing on a new agent was a manual, multi-step effort — delaying the moment they could begin earning.

Scattered information

Circulars, guidance and training lived in fragmented places, so agents struggled to find what they needed to stay compliant.

My role & process

I led the design of the platform from first principles to final UI, working closely with the client to understand a complex, regulated, role-based operation and turn it into something people could actually use. My contribution spanned four fronts:

Led the design end to end

Owned the experience from research and structure through to the final, build-ready interface.

Mapped a complex domain

Worked alongside the client to learn the agent lifecycle, licensing rules and how each role really works.

Designed for many roles

Shaped distinct experiences for the client's staff and for agents — all from one coherent system.

Built a scalable system

Created a responsive design system so the product stays consistent as new roles and features are added.

Two kinds of users

One platform, two very different jobs to do.

The platform quietly serves two very different audiences from the same foundation. The client's back-office staff use it to run and oversee the agent network; the agents use it to run their own businesses. Who you are decides what you see — so role-based access shaped every screen and every flow from the start.

Internal · Back office

Run and oversee the network

Staff own the agent network end to end — turning paperwork and follow-up calls into clear, trackable actions.

  • Manage agents from one view of the whole network
  • Onboard agents through a guided online flow
  • Track applications from submitted to settled
  • Approve or reject, with a record of every decision
  • Promote agents through the hierarchy

Agent

Run their own business

Agents get their network, their customers and their earnings in one place, tailored to their role.

  • See their agent hierarchy and downline
  • Their customers and the transactions behind them
  • Track licences and renewals to stay compliant
  • Onboard new downline agents online
  • Circulars — official notices in one place
  • An info centre and a training module
  • Business overview and commission statements

Key design decisions

A few decisions shaped the whole product. Each one traces straight back to a problem in the old way of working.

01

One system, many tailored views

Rather than separate tools, a single role-based platform shows each person only what their role needs — simpler to use, and easier to maintain.

02

Online, trackable flows over paper

In-person form-filling became guided online flows that leave a visible trail — so work can be followed, measured and audited.

03

Less manual, repetitive work

Sensible defaults and automation remove the repeated steps the old system forced — freeing staff and agents to spend time where it matters.

Final UI

The final interface brings this together in a calm, confident system — clear enough to carry a regulated, role-heavy product without overwhelming the people using it.

Outcome

The result is an operation that finally runs online and can be seen into. Onboarding and routine requests that once meant paper, travel and repeated manual steps now happen in guided digital flows that staff and agents can track from start to finish. In place of fifteen-year-old software, the client now has a modern, responsive foundation built to grow with the business and support new roles and features over time.

The qualitative shift is clear; hard numbers on time saved, onboarding speed and volumes are best drawn from the client's own data once the platform has been live long enough to measure. Add real metrics here once available.

Conclusion

The hardest part of this project wasn't how it looked, but how much it had to hold: many roles, strict rules, and a business that couldn't afford confusion. The work was to make all of that feel calm and obvious — so that whether you're approving an application or growing your agency, the next step is always clear. That's the craft I care about most: bringing order and clarity to complex, regulated products.
